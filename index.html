Cockney Crazy!

In this trivia game you will guess the translations of Cockney Rhyming Slang! What's Cockney Ryhming Slang? Glad you asked! It is, according to Wikipedia, a form of slang word construction in the English language that uses rhyme. It started in the early 19th century in the East End of London. Probably the best and most widely known slang term is apples and pears translating to stairs. So, for example, if you asked, "Where did John go?", the response would be, "Up the apples and pears", meaning, John went up the stairs.
